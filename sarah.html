What is Christmas?

Christmas is a major annual holiday (Dec 25th) celebrating the birth of Jesus Christ, central to Christianity, but also a global cultural event with secular traditions like gift-giving, feasting, and Santa Claus, blending Christian nativity stories with ancient winter solstice festivals. Christians commemorate Jesus's arrival as the savior, while others enjoy the festive spirit, family time, and winter traditions.

  • What is Christmas
    • For Christians, it marks the birth of Jesus, the Son of God, symbolizing hope, salvation, and God's love for humanity.
    • It's a widely celebrated festival with secular traditions like decorating trees, exchanging gifts, festive meals, and Santa Claus, becoming a global cultural and commercial event.
  • Why is it Celebrated
    • To honor the birth of Jesus, whose story involves angels, shepherds, wise men (Magi), and a journey to Bethlehem, as described in the Bible.
    • The December 25th date aligns with existing Roman winter solstice festivals (like Saturnalia) celebrating the return of longer days, a practice later adopted by Christianity.
    • Traditions/Customs
      • Nativity Scenes - Recreating the manger scene.
      • Gift Giving - Symbolizes the gifts of the Magi and generosity.
      • Decorations - Christmas trees (from pagan winter symbols) and lights.
      • Family & Community - Feasting, caroling, and gatherings.
  • Key Figures & Stories
    • Jesus Christ - Born in Bethlehem; believed by Christians to be the Messiah.
    • Mary & Joseph - His parents, guided by divine messages.
    • Shepherds & Magi - The first visitors, bringing gifts.
    • Santa Claus - A figure associated with gift-giving, linked to St. Nicholas and folklore.

How to Celebrate (in the US/Canada)

  1. Before Christmas
    1. Plan Ahead - Start decorating, shopping, and planning meals early to reduce stress.
    2. Decorate - Put up a Christmas tree with lights and ornaments, and decorate your home inside and out.
    3. Advent Calendar - Use an Advent calendar to count down the days to Christmas.
    4. Bake & Cook - Make holiday treats like gingerbread houses or cookies.
    5. Create a Festive Atmosphere - Listen to Christmas music, drive around to see lights, or make cards.
    6. Gift Shopping - Buy presents for friends and family, or consider charitable giving.
  2. Christmas Eve
    1. Special Meal/Snacks - Enjoy hot chocolate and cookies.
    2. Bake cookies for Santa.
    3. Read - Read holiday stories or the Nativity story.
    4. Santa Prep - Leave out the cookies and milk for Santa.
    5. Go to Church (Optional)
  3. Chrismas Day
    1. Church (Optional) - Attend a Christmas Day service.
    2. Gift Exchange - Exchange gifts, often after opening stockings.
    3. Festive Meal - Prepare and share a special Christmas lunch or dinner.
    4. Family Time - Play games, watch holiday movies, or go for a walk.
    5. Connect - Call or video chat with loved ones far away.
