Thanksgiving

Thanksgiving was first celebrated on autumn of 1621 in Massachusetts. Thanksgiving is celebrated when people come together and feast on food. A traditional Thanksgiving is started of with a prayer and is filled with Turkey, Mash Patatoes, and Gravy!. Thanksgiving is celebrated to mark to commemorate the first successful harvest feast shared by Plymouth colonists and the Wampanoag people in 1621, and to express gratitude for the blessings of the past year

Where is it Celebrated

  • United States of America
  • Cannada
  • United Kingdom

Steps on how it is Celebrated

  1. Travel: Many people travel long distances to be with loved ones, making the days leading up to Thanksgiving some of the busiest travel periods of the year.
  2. Cooking & Meal Planning: Preparing the elaborate meal often begins a day or two in advance.
  3. Giving Back: In the spirit of giving thanks and helping others, some people donate food to local food banks or volunteer to serve meals to the poor.
  4. Football: A traditional thing to do during Thanksgiving is to watch Football but not all families follow this tradition.
  5. Eat!: During the evening people eat Turkey and other foods (some people exchange food) before eating some families say a prayer saying what they are greatfull for!.
