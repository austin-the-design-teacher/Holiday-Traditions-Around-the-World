Thanksgiving

What is Thanksgiving?

Thanksgiving is a U.S. federal holiday on the fourth Thursday of November, rooted in a 1621 harvest feast shared by Plymouth colonists (Pilgrims) and the Wampanoag people, celebrated as a time to give thanks for blessings, family, and bounty, though it's now largely secular, marked by large meals, traditions like turkey and pie, and community giving, with evolving historical perspectives including Native American viewpoints.

  • How the holiday is celebrated in the USA
    1. Gather: People travel to gather with family and friends.
    2. Cook: Together, they prepare a large, traditional meal like turkey and side dishes.
    3. Eat: Everyone sits down together, sharing what they are thankful for before eating.
    4. Relax: The evening is spent relaxing, watching football, and playing games
